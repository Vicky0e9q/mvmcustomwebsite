How to Pick Exterior Paint Colors

John Smith , January 1st, 2017

Painting the outside of your home and choosing which colors will boost your curb appeal can be daunting. The last thing you want is to pick a color palette that will clash with your roof, trim, or other structural details.


But don’t worry — the painting pros at Five Star Painting® have put together a helpful guide on how to pick the perfect exterior paint colors for your home

Tip 1: Think About Your Home’s Architectural Style


It’s important to consider the architectural style of your home before opting for a specific exterior paint color. This is because some home styles pair better with certain color palettes than others. A Victorian home, for example, may stand out more when combined with vibrant, bold colors while a minimalist, modern residence might benefit from neutral colors or a more monochromatic scheme.

Tip 2: Evaluate Your Landscape


Take in your surroundings, including your landscaping, the neighboring homes, and other exterior details. This can help you narrow down which colors will work best with the surrounding environment to create a cohesive look.

Tip 3: Study Home Materials


Examine your home’s exterior materials and their texture (e.g., roof tiles, bricks, and stones) when considering which color palette to choose. Finding exterior shades and colors that complement existing materials can tie your home’s exterior nicely together.

Tip 4: Assess Architectural Details


Consider architectural elements like windows, doors, trim, and other details to gauge whether you want them to blend in or stand out. Then, select colors that highlight or complement these architectural details to create a balanced look.

Tip 5: Compare Colors in Different Lighting


Paint colors typically look different in different lighting conditions, such as morning, afternoon, evening, and night. We recommend testing sample swatches of your desired colors under different lighting before settling on the right one, especially since natural light will affect how specific colors appear on the exterior of your home.

Tip 6: Gauge Roof Color


Your roof color is a crucial factor to consider when picking out exterior house colors, as you don’t want to choose a color that clashes with it. A good rule of thumb is to pick a paint color that’s a few shades lighter or darker to create an inviting contrast.
